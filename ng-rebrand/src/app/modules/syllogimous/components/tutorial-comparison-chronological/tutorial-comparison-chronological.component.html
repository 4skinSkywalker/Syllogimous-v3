Comparison Chronological

Comparison chronological questions challenge your ability to determine the correct order of events or objects based on the given premises. Here's how you can navigate these puzzles:

Step 1

Each question starts with a series of statements that describe the relative order of items. For example:

  • Premise 1: Knife is after Shoulder
  • Premise 2: Knife is before Hostel
  • Premise 3: Desktop is after Hostel
  • Premise 4: Knife is after Desktop

These premises provide the necessary information to establish the chronological sequence of the items.

Step 2

You need to mentally or visually map out the order of the items based on the premises. Here's how you can break it down:

  1. Identify Direct Relationships:
    • "Knife is after Shoulder" means Shoulder comes before Knife.
    • "Knife is before Hostel" means Knife comes before Hostel.
  2. Cross-reference with Other Premises:
    • "Desktop is after Hostel" places Desktop after Hostel.
    • "Knife is after Desktop" implies that Knife comes after Desktop.

Step 3

Using the premises, arrange the items in the correct sequence:

  • From Premise 2 and Premise 3: Shoulder → Knife → Hostel → Desktop.
  • However, Premise 4 contradicts Premise 2 and Premise 3. Premise 4 says "Knife is after Desktop," which cannot logically fit into the sequence established by the previous premises.

This discrepancy shows that there's an inconsistency, indicating that not all premises can be true simultaneously.

Step 4

Based on your analysis, you may be asked to verify a statement like "Is the Knife correctly positioned after the Desktop?" Given the contradictory premises, you would determine that this sequence is False.

After selecting True or False, you'll receive feedback, allowing you to see if your reasoning was correct.

Tips for Success

  • Visualize the sequence: Drawing a timeline or diagram can help you more easily identify the correct order.
  • Watch for contradictions: Some questions are designed to include contradictory premises to test your attention to detail.

By following these steps, you can confidently tackle comparison chronological questions, improving your ability to logically order events or items based on given information!
