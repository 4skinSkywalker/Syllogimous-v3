How to Play Direction

Step 1

Each question will present a series of directional relationships. These premises describe where one object is located in relation to another. For example:

  • Premise 1: Jewelry is at East of Color
  • Premise 2: Tail is at West of Milk
  • Premise 3: Color is at South-East of Tail
  • Premise 4: Milk is at South-East of Jewelry

These premises establish the spatial relationships between the different objects.

Step 2

To solve the puzzle, you need to mentally (or physically) map out the positions of each object based on the given premises.

  1. Start with a base reference: Choose one object to start with. For example, place "Tail" in your mental map or on paper.
  2. Place the related objects: According to the premises:
    • If "Tail is at West of Milk," then place "Milk" to the right (east) of "Tail."
    • "Color is at South-East of Tail," so "Color" should be below and to the right of "Tail."
    • "Jewelry is at East of Color," so "Jewelry" should be directly to the right of "Color."
    • "Milk is at South-East of Jewelry," meaning "Milk" is positioned below and to the right of "Jewelry."

Step 3

Once you have a clear map of the objects, check the consistency of their relationships according to the premises. For example, confirm that "Milk" is indeed south-east of "Jewelry" and that "Jewelry" is correctly placed east of "Color."

Step 4

Based on the spatial arrangement you've mapped out, you might be asked to determine the validity of a specific directional statement. If the question provides a new statement like "Jewelry is north of Tail," you would compare it against your mental map.

If your map shows that "Jewelry" is not north of "Tail" but rather to its east, you would click False. If the statement matches your map, click True.

After selecting either True or False, the game will indicate whether your choice was correct. This helps reinforce your understanding of spatial relationships and improve your problem-solving skills.

The Negation Cue

Whenever you encounter a phrase highlighted in red, it's an indication that you need to think inversely about the information presented.

Tips for Success

  • Draw it out: If you find it challenging to visualize the positions, draw them on paper to keep track of the objects and their relationships.
  • Double-check your map: Before making your final choice, review the entire map to ensure accuracy.

By following these steps, you'll enhance your ability to solve direction-type questions, making you better at spatial reasoning and logical thinking.
