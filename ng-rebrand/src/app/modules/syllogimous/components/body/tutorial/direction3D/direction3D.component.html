How to Play Direction3D

Direction3D questions are spatial reasoning puzzles where you must determine the relative positions of different objects in a 3D space based on given statements. Hereâ€™s how you can play:

Step 1

Each Direction3D question presents a set of spatial relationships between different objects. For example:

  • Statement 1: Temple is Above and West of Luggage
  • Statement 2: Luggage is South of Governor
  • Statement 3: Hotel is North-East of Temple
  • Statement 4: Governor is Below of Hotel

These statements describe the positions of objects relative to one another in three-dimensional space.

Step 2

To solve the puzzle, try to mentally visualize or sketch the positions of the objects based on the given statements:

  • Temple is positioned Above and West of Luggage.
  • Luggage is positioned South of Governor.
  • Hotel is positioned North-East of Temple.
  • Governor is positioned Below Hotel.

Step 3

After understanding each object's relative position, you may be asked to verify a specific relationship between two or more objects. For example:

Question: "Is the Hotel above the Governor?"

Based on the given statements:

  • Hotel is North-East of Temple, and Governor is Below Hotel.
  • Therefore, Hotel should indeed be above Governor.

Step 4

Based on your analysis, click the True button if you believe the given relationship is correct or the False button if you think it is not.

For instance:

If the game asks if "Hotel is above the Governor," you would select True based on the reasoning above.

After making your choice, the game will confirm whether you were correct or not, helping you refine your spatial reasoning skills.

The Negation Cue

Whenever you encounter a phrase highlighted in red, it's an indication that you need to think inversely about the information presented.

Tips for Success

Use a mental map: Visualize or draw a simple sketch to track the positions of each object.

Consider all dimensions: Remember that positions can change along multiple axes (north-south, east-west, above-below).
