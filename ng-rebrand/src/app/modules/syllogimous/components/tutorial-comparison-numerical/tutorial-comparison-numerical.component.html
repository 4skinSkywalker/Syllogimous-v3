How to Play Comparison Numerical

Step 1

Each question presents you with a set of comparisons between different items. For example:

  • Comparison 1: Sea is less than Pyramid
  • Comparison 2: Sea is more than Turtle
  • Comparison 3: Wrist is less than Turtle
  • Comparison 4: Pyramid is more than Turtle

These comparisons provide the relational framework needed to evaluate a conclusion.

Step 2

The key to solving these questions is understanding how the items relate to each other based on the given comparisons. Letâ€™s break down the example:

  • Sea < Pyramid: The Sea is smaller (or less) than the Pyramid.
  • Sea > Turtle: The Sea is larger (or more) than the Turtle.
  • Wrist < Turtle: The Wrist is smaller (or less) than the Turtle.
  • Pyramid > Turtle: The Pyramid is larger (or more) than the Turtle.

Step 3

You will need to determine if a new comparison or conclusion is logically valid based on the relationships given. For example:

Conclusion: "Wrist is less than Sea."

To evaluate this conclusion, consider the chain of relationships:

Since Sea is more than Turtle and Wrist is less than Turtle, it logically follows that Wrist is less than Sea.

Step 4

After analyzing the relationships:

  • If the conclusion logically follows from the given comparisons, click the True button.
  • If the conclusion does not logically follow, click the False button.

For the example provided:

Conclusion: "Wrist is less than Sea."

Based on the comparisons (Wrist < Turtle < Sea), this conclusion would be True.

After submitting your answer, the game will provide feedback on whether your choice was correct, helping you learn and improve your ability to evaluate numerical comparisons.

Tips for Success

  • Map it out: Sometimes it helps to visualize the relationships in a linear order or hierarchy.
  • Consider all premises: Ensure that your conclusion is consistent with all the given comparisons.
  • Practice and patience: With practice, you'll improve your ability to quickly analyze and draw accurate conclusions.
