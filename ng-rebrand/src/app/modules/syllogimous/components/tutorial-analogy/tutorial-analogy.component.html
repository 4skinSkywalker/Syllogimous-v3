How to Play Analogy

Step 1

Each analogy question begins with a set of statements that define the relationships between different items. For example:

  • Statement 1: Brush is the same as Desk
  • Statement 2: Desk is opposite of Bread
  • Statement 3: Skirt is opposite of Heart
  • Statement 4: Bread is the same as Skirt

These statements provide the groundwork for understanding how each item relates to the others.

Step 2

After reviewing the relationships, you'll be presented with an analogy that you need to evaluate. For example:

Analogy to Evaluate: "Heart to Bread is the same as Skirt to Desk."

Your task is to determine whether the relationship between "Heart and Bread" is equivalent to the relationship between "Skirt and Desk."

Step 3

To solve the analogy, follow these steps:

  1. Identify the relationships between the items in each pair.
    • From Statement 3: "Skirt is opposite of Heart."
    • From Statement 4: "Bread is the same as Skirt."
    • From Statement 2: "Desk is opposite of Bread."
  2. Map the relationships to the analogy:
    • "Heart to Bread": Since "Bread is the same as Skirt" and "Skirt is opposite of Heart," the relationship here is one of opposition.
    • "Skirt to Desk": Since "Bread is the same as Skirt" and "Desk is opposite of Bread," the relationship here is also one of opposition.
  3. Determine consistency: Both "Heart to Bread" and "Skirt to Desk" have an "opposite" relationship. Therefore, the analogy is consistent.

Step 4

After analyzing the relationships, you need to decide whether the analogy holds true:

  • If the relationships match, click True.
  • If they do not match, click False.

In this example, since both pairs have an opposite relationship, you would select True.

Once you've made your selection, the game will give you feedback, helping you to learn from your choices and improve your ability to recognize patterns in analogies.

Tips for Success

  • Break down the relationships: Focus on one pair at a time to avoid confusion.
  • Practice regularly: Familiarity with different types of relationships will help you solve analogies more quickly and accurately.

By following these steps, you'll be better equipped to tackle analogy questions, improving your pattern recognition and logical reasoning skills!
